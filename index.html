  • Plants: Anubias, Java Fern, Frogbit for cover & enrichment.
  • Water flow: gentle; mimic slow rivers.

Tank Setup & Water Parameters

Plan First: Species ‚Üí Water Type

  • Match water to the puffer: Pea (freshwater), Figure-8 (low-end brackish for life), Green Spotted (brackish as juvenile; many adults near-marine).
  • Pick tank size by species: Pea ~5 gal for one; Figure-8 ~15‚Äì20 gal; Green Spotted ~30 gal (more room = easier care).

Bigger than the minimum is always better for puffers.

Core Gear

  • Filtration: Puffers are messy‚Äîuse a filter rated ~2√ó your tank volume (canister/HOB; add sponge for bio + gentle flow).
  • Heater: aim ~74‚Äì82¬∞F (23‚Äì28¬∞C) depending on species; use a reliable thermostat (two smaller heaters = redundancy).
  • Lighting: moderate; choose a full-spectrum LED if keeping live plants.
  • Lid: tight cover; puffers can jump when startled.
  • Test kits: ammonia, nitrite, nitrate, pH; add refractometer for brackish/marine.

Substrate & Aquascape (Step-by-Step)

  1. Substrate: fine sand for most puffers; if doing a planted scape, lay aquasoil and slope it higher in the back for depth.
  2. Hardscape: add rock/driftwood; use natural stones/woods and place using the ‚Äúrule of thirds‚Äù for balance.
  3. Plants: add hardy species (Anubias, Java Fern, Crypts) and mist while planting; attach rhizomes to wood/rock.
  4. Fill gently: dechlorinated water; pour over a plate/bag to avoid clouding the layout.

Water Targets (by type)

  • Freshwater (e.g., Pea): pH ~6.8‚Äì7.6; 74‚Äì78¬∞F; NH3/NO2=0; NO3<20 ppm.
  • Brackish (e.g., Figure-8, juvenile GSP): pH ~7.5‚Äì8.2; 75‚Äì82¬∞F; SG ~1.005‚Äì1.010.
  • Near-Marine/Marine (adult GSP, marine puffers): pH ~8.1‚Äì8.4; 75‚Äì82¬∞F; SG ~1.020‚Äì1.025.

Cycle Before Fish (4‚Äì6 weeks typical)

  1. Run all equipment; add an ammonia source (pure ammonia or fish food).
  2. Test every few days; wait until ammonia & nitrite = 0 ppm and nitrate is present.
  3. Do a big water change to lower nitrate; then add the puffer.

Daily checks: temp & gear. Weekly: 25‚Äì30% water change, glass clean, test log. Monthly: rinse filter media in tank water (not tap).

Daily & Weekly Care

Daily

  • Quick health scan: clear eyes, smooth breathing, active but not frantic.
  • Feed appropriate foods (see below); remove uneaten bits.
  • Top off evaporated water (freshwater for brackish/marine tanks).

Weekly

  • Water change 20‚Äì40% depending on bioload.
  • Gravel vac/light sand stir; clean glass.
  • Test water; log results to spot trends.

Diet & Enrichment

Puffers are carnivores with strong beaks that keep growing. They need crunchy foods to wear teeth down.

  • Staples: snails, shrimp, krill, bloodworms, clams/mussels (appropriately sized).
  • Crunch factor: offer hard-shelled foods regularly to prevent overgrowth.
  • Variety: rotate foods; avoid exclusively ‚Äúsoft‚Äù frozen fare.
  • Enrichment: tongs feeding, scatter feeding, safe decor to explore.

Avoid feeder fish; they can introduce disease and poor nutrition.

Health & Safety

  • Acclimate slowly; avoid rapid shifts in salinity or temperature.
  • Quarantine new livestock when possible.
  • Toxin note: Some puffers carry tetrodotoxin internally‚Äîadmire, don‚Äôt handle.
  • Stress: A puffed puffer is stressed; focus on calm, clean water.
