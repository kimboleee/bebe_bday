Tank Setup & Water Parameters

Plan First: Species ‚Üí Water Type

  • Match water to the puffer: Pea (freshwater), Figure-8 (low-end brackish for life), Green Spotted (brackish as juvenile; many adults near-marine).
  • Pick tank size by species: Pea ~5 gal for one; Figure-8 ~15‚Äì20 gal; Green Spotted ~30 gal (more room = easier care).

Bigger than the minimum is always better for puffers.

Core Gear

  • Filtration: Puffers are messy‚Äîuse a filter rated ~2√ó your tank volume (canister/HOB; add sponge for bio + gentle flow).
  • Heater: aim ~74‚Äì82¬∞F (23‚Äì28¬∞C) depending on species; use a reliable thermostat (two smaller heaters = redundancy).
  • Lighting: moderate; choose a full-spectrum LED if keeping live plants.
  • Lid: tight cover; puffers can jump when startled.
  • Test kits: ammonia, nitrite, nitrate, pH; add refractometer for brackish/marine.

Substrate & Aquascape (Step-by-Step)

  1. Substrate: fine sand for most puffers; if doing a planted scape, lay aquasoil and slope it higher in the back for depth.
  2. Hardscape: add rock/driftwood; use natural stones/woods and place using the ‚Äúrule of thirds‚Äù for balance.
  3. Plants: add hardy species (Anubias, Java Fern, Crypts) and mist while planting; attach rhizomes to wood/rock.
  4. Fill gently: dechlorinated water; pour over a plate/bag to avoid clouding the layout.

Water Targets (by type)

  • Freshwater (e.g., Pea): pH ~6.8‚Äì7.6; 74‚Äì78¬∞F; NH3/NO2=0; NO3<20 ppm.
  • Brackish (e.g., Figure-8, juvenile GSP): pH ~7.5‚Äì8.2; 75‚Äì82¬∞F; SG ~1.005‚Äì1.010.
  • Near-Marine/Marine (adult GSP, marine puffers): pH ~8.1‚Äì8.4; 75‚Äì82¬∞F; SG ~1.020‚Äì1.025.

Cycle Before Fish (4‚Äì6 weeks typical)

  1. Run all equipment; add an ammonia source (pure ammonia or fish food).
  2. Test every few days; wait until ammonia & nitrite = 0 ppm and nitrate is present.
  3. Do a big water change to lower nitrate; then add the puffer.

Daily checks: temp & gear. Weekly: 25‚Äì30% water change, glass clean, test log. Monthly: rinse filter media in tank water (not tap).

Acclimation (Arrival Day)

  1. Lights down: Dim the room/tank lights to lower stress.
  2. Float to equalize temp: Place the sealed bag in the tank for 15‚Äì20 minutes.
  3. Check the water type: If possible, quickly test the bag water (pH / salinity). This tells you how different it is from your tank.
  4. Freshwater method: Open the bag and add a small cup of tank water every 5‚Äì10 minutes for ~20‚Äì30 minutes, repeating several times.
  5. Brackish / Marine (preferred drip): Use a clean airline tube with a loose knot/valve to drip tank water into the bag (about 2‚Äì4 drops/sec). Continue 45‚Äì60 minutes until salinity (SG) in the bag matches the tank.
  6. Never pour store water into your tank: When acclimated, gently net the puffer into the tank and discard the bag water.
  7. Go slow, lights low: Leave lights dim for an hour. Don‚Äôt feed the first day; resume with small portions once settled.
  8. Observe: Watch breathing, posture, and swimming. Have a quarantine/hospital container ready just in case.

Daily & Weekly Care

Daily

  • Feed smart: species-appropriate meaty foods; only what‚Äôs eaten in ~2‚Äì3 minutes; remove leftovers.
  • Equipment check: verify heater temp and filter flow; lights on a consistent schedule.

Weekly

  • Top-off vs. changes: top-off = freshwater; water change = premixed saltwater at matched SG/temp.
  • Buffering: aragonite/crushed coral can help hold pH/KH for higher-pH species.

Quick Troubleshoot

Diet & Enrichment

What to Feed

  • Staples: snails, shrimp, krill, bloodworms, clams/mussels, cockle, squid (species-appropriate sizes).
  • Crunch factor: offer hard-shelled items regularly to keep teeth worn down (aim 2‚Äì3√ó/week).
  • Variety: rotate foods; thaw frozen food in tank water, never microwave; avoid flakes as a ‚Äúmain diet.‚Äù
  • Treats: rich items (e.g., fatty shellfish) in moderation to avoid water fouling/obesity.

  • Adult schedule: feed once daily or every other day depending on species/metabolism.
  • Portion size: only what they‚Äôll finish in ~2‚Äì3 minutes; remove leftovers promptly.
  • Juveniles/smaller species: smaller, more frequent meals are fine‚Äîwatch body condition, not begging.

Health & Safety

Best Practices

  • Acclimate gently: float to match temp, then add small amounts of tank water; for brackish/marine, use a drip until salinity matches. Never add bag water to the tank.
  • Water quality first: keep ammonia/nitrite at 0; nitrate low with regular changes; match pH & salinity to species.
  • Quarantine when possible: observe new fish for parasites/disease before adding to the display tank.
  • Teeth & diet: schedule crunchy foods weekly to prevent dental overgrowth.
  • Stress signals: rapid breathing, clamped fins, hiding, refusal to eat‚Äîrecheck parameters and reduce flow/lighting.
  • Handling: avoid; admire only. Use a container (not a net) if you must move the fish.

  • Near-Marine/Marine (adult GSP & many marine puffers): pH ~8.1‚Äì8.4; 75‚Äì82¬∞F; SG ~1.020‚Äì1.025.

Stability beats perfection‚Äîavoid fast swings in salinity, pH, or temperature.
